## Failure Modes (and how to recover)

### Week 1 went poorly — 0 replies to 30 DMs
- DMs too generic? Re-read Script 1 in 03, personalize each by 1 detail
- Wrong audience? Filter LinkedIn for HK construction firm size + role correctly
- Timing? Re-send Tue/Wed afternoon (best for HK B2B)

### Week 2 — got demo bookings but they cancelled
- Calendar tool issue? Send Zoom link directly in WhatsApp, not Calendly
- They forgot? Send WhatsApp 2 hours before meeting
- Reschedule once. If 2nd no-show → drop to Cold list. Don't beg.

### Week 3 — pilot started but they're not using it
- Day 3-5: WhatsApp check-in ("setup OK?")
- Day 5-7: Offer 15-min walkthrough via Zoom
- If still no usage by Day 14: discuss whether to extend pilot OR end. Honest convo.

### Week 4 — pilot active but won't convert to paid
- Ask exactly why. Not "think about it" — get specific.
- If price → offer founding discount + annual prepaid.
- If feature → note as roadmap input, set 60-day re-touch.
- If "not now" → keep at HK$0 for 1 more month with explicit Day 60 decision date in writing.

## Month 2-3 (after first 30 days)

If Month 1 hits 3 customers:
- Month 2 target: 5 new customers (8 total). Same playbook, but use Month 1 case studies.
- Month 3 target: 8 new customers (16 total). Hire VA or PT for outreach. Run paid ads.

If Month 1 hits 1-2 customers:
- Same plan. Don't pivot prematurely.
- Refine 1 area: better demo? Better pitch? Better follow-up?

If Month 1 hits 0 customers:
- Stop and review. Talk to 5 "no" decision makers. Ask honest why.
- May need product change OR pricing change OR target shift (T1 → T2 maybe).
- This is normal at month 1. Don't quit.

## Top 3 things to remember

1. **Pilot is the close**. Once they say yes to pilot, you're 75% won. Don't overcomplicate.
2. **Listen, then pitch**. Their pain quote = your slide 3. Use their words back.
3. **Show, don't tell**. Demo > slides. iPhone in hand > screenshot.
